Water Pollution

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies (e.g.lakes,rivers, oceans,aquifers and groundwater).Water pollution occurs when pollutants are directly or indirectly discharged into water bodies without adequate treatment to remove harmful compounds.

Type of Water Pollution Contributing Factor Causes Harm To Remedial Measure
Chemical pollution These occur in industrial places and activities such as oil refineries, coal power plants, construction, mining & smelting, transportation, agricultural use of pesticides and insecticides, as well as household activities Chemical pollution can affect the delicate balance of the Earth's ecosystems. Mining, agriculture and waste disposal have caused substantial soil pollution. The presence of heavy metals like cadmium, mercury and lead can affect soil quality and reduce the number of micro-organisms that support soil fertility Recycle, reuse or donate liquids from automobiles. Do not pour them down the drain or throw them away in the regular trash
Groundwater pollution Apart from septic systems, chemicals, mining, landfills, animal wastes, and road runoff, the two primary anthropogenic drivers of pollution of groundwater are fertilizer application and inappropriate industrial waste handling Drinking contaminated groundwater can have serious health effects. Diseases such as hepatitis and dysentery may be caused by contamination from septic tank waste Groundwater remediation is the process that is used to treat polluted groundwater by removing the pollutants or converting them into harmless products.
